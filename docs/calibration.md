# Calibration and concordance

DraftLens has two separate evaluation questions:

1. **Authorship benchmark validity:** can the estimator separate known human and machine-generated samples at a conservative false-positive operating point?
2. **Turnitin concordance:** does its qualifying-text denominator, score display, and passage coverage behave similarly to observable Turnitin reports?

These questions must not be collapsed. A detector can resemble another detector without establishing who wrote a document.

## Publicly documented score semantics

The version 2 pipeline mirrors only public product semantics:

- analyze long-form prose rather than every word in a file;
- exclude bibliography and common non-prose structures;
- score overlapping local windows of roughly 5-10 sentences;
- aggregate detected qualifying-word coverage;
- show `0%` for no detected coverage, suppress raw 1-19% results as `*%`, and show exact results from 20-100%;
- require 300-30,000 qualifying words.

Sources: [Turnitin AI Writing Report guide](https://guides.turnitin.com/hc/en-us/articles/22774058814093-Using-the-AI-Writing-Report), [Turnitin AI detection FAQ](https://guides.turnitin.com/hc/en-us/articles/28477544839821-Turnitin-s-AI-writing-detection-capabilities-FAQs), and [model release history](https://guides.turnitin.com/hc/en-us/articles/28294949544717-AI-writing-detection-model).

Turnitin does not publish its checkpoint, training corpus, sentence threshold, calibration formula, or exact aggregation rule. DraftLens therefore cannot reproduce the proprietary classifier and does not claim score equivalence.

## Current statistical profile

`ghostbuster-essay-v2` is a standardized logistic model over 22 passage-level style features. It was trained with the openly licensed [Ghostbuster essay corpus](https://github.com/vivek3141/ghostbuster-data) (CC BY 3.0). Prompt/file IDs are kept in one split to prevent the same essay topic leaking across training and test sets. Source essays are not shipped with the app.

Held-out profile metrics:

| Metric | Result |
| --- | ---: |
| Test documents | 1,140 |
| Window ROC-AUC | 0.926 |
| Window balanced accuracy | 0.756 |
| Human documents at or above the 20% reporting line | 0.61% |
| AI-document recall at that operating point | 64.99% |

These are corpus-specific measurements, not real-world guarantees. The threshold intentionally accepts more false negatives to reduce reportable human false positives.

The profile is generated by `scripts/train-calibration.mjs`; coefficients, source revision, split policy, threshold, and validation metrics are stored in `src/data/calibration-profile.ts`.

## Release gates for stronger concordance claims

Before describing a future profile as Turnitin-concordant, it should meet all of these on held-out documents:

- at least 40 provenance-checked reports across four domains;
- mean absolute score error no greater than 10 points;
- median absolute error no greater than 8 points;
- Spearman score correlation of at least 0.75;
- at least 90% of documents within 20 score points;
- no more than 5% of verified-human documents crossing the displayed 20% line;
- passage-level detected-word F1 of at least 0.60 where highlight maps are available.

Every paired label must retain report date and model period because Turnitin updates are not retroactive. Suppressed `*%` results are interval-censored 1-19, never relabeled as zero.
